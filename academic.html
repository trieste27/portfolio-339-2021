Public Policy 320: Politics & Public Policy

Decision Memo 3

In 2019, New York State passed the Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act (CLCPA). This nation-leading legislation established a 22-member Climate Action Council to form a Scoping Plan that achieves the CLCPA's stringent goals for reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

In this decision memo, I advise the Council to take a market-oriented approach to emissions reduction and build an economy-wide cap-and-trade program in New York State. When considering the actors involved with the implementation of California's cap-and-trade system (running since 2009) and the susceptibility of state-level executive agencies to business interests, it is clear that a flexible market mechanism is the politically feasible policy solution.

Public Policy 320: Politics & Public Policy

Decision Memo 1

Writing as a policy advisor to the director of the Political Reform program at New America, I identified fundamental institutional reforms which may profoundly affect how politics and policymaking function in the U.S. today.

In my first attempt at writing in the decision memorandum format, I argue for a dual approach to electoral reform through open, top-four primaries with ranked choice voting. This can restore electoral competition in the U.S. and redistribute the power of the political duopoly.

Public Policy 320: Politics & Public Policy

Decision Memo 2

This decision memo addresses the ongoing debate regarding school funding reform in Connecticut. Writing from the perspective of an advisor to the Connecticut General Assembly, I was tasked with offering input on Michigan's Proposal A, a 1994 funding reform that shifted the base of revenues from local property taxes to state sales taxes.

I argue in favor of a similar policy change in CT so as to equalize tax revenues across districts and, ultimately, uncouple family background from access to quality public education. Though it is a major change from the status quo, tax reform is the institutional means for far-reaching change.

Public Policy 201: Systematic Thinking About Problems of the Day

Op-Ed

In this op-ed, I advocate for the use of federal funding for a Universal Eviction Moratorium “Plus.” This policy takes care of low-income renters during the pandemic-induced economic crisis, and addresses the discrimination which historically barred some Americans from the housing market. Further, this policy is an immediate response which satisfies the broader, long-term goals of the Biden Administration.

American Culture 293: 20th & 21st Century Writing by U.S. Women of Color

Midterm Paper

In this paper I discuss racial inequities in access to U.S. housing, as indicated by language techniques and character narratives in Toni Morrison's "The Bluest Eye."
